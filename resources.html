Biology 101 Notes

1. Introduction to Biology

Meaning of Biology

  • Biology is the branch of science that studies living organisms and their interactions with one another and with their environment.
  • The word Biology comes from: Bios = life, Logos = study. So, biology literally means ‚Äúthe study of life.‚Äù

Importance of Biology

  • Learn how organisms grow, reproduce, and adapt.
  • Discover how living things interact with the environment (ecology).
  • Improve medicine, agriculture, and industry through biological knowledge.
  • Explain human life processes like digestion, respiration, and reproduction.

2. Characteristics of Living Things (MRS GREN)

All living things share certain features that distinguish them from non-living things. These characteristics can be remembered using the acronym MRS GREN:

  • Movement: Ability to change position or part of the body.
  • Respiration: Breakdown of food to release energy.
  • Sensitivity: Ability to detect and respond to changes.
  • Growth: Permanent increase in size and complexity.
  • Reproduction: Process to produce new individuals.
  • Excretion: Removal of toxic waste products.
  • Nutrition: Process of obtaining and using food.

3. The Cell - Basic Unit of Life

Cell Theory

  1. All living things are made up of one or more cells.
  2. The cell is the basic unit of structure and function of life.
  3. All cells arise from pre-existing cells.

Types of Cells

  • Prokaryotic cells: Simple, no true nucleus (e.g., Bacteria).
  • Eukaryotic cells: Complex, with a true nucleus (e.g., Plant and animal cells).

Cell Structures (Organelles) and Functions

  • Nucleus: Controls activities, contains DNA.
  • Mitochondria: Releases energy (ATP).
  • Ribosomes: Protein synthesis.
  • Cell wall (plants): Provides support and protection.
  • Chloroplasts (plants): Site of photosynthesis.
